The coca

What is a coca

In Algeria , a coca is a traditional savory pie or "covered pizza" stuffed with frita (a mix of onions, tomatoes, and peppers) and topped with a black olive. It is a staple Mediterranean street food.

Type of coca

Frita

The classic version (onions, tomatoes, and bell peppers).

Trim

How to make a coca

Preparation

The dough

  • 3 ½ cups: 450g flour:
  • ¾ cup + 1 tablespoon: 200ml milk
  • ⅓ cup + 1 tablespoon: 100ml oil
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tablespoon milk
  • Melted margarine

The stuffing

  • 3 large onions, chopped
  • 1 cup fresh or crushed tomatoes (200g)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon pepper
  • Black olives (optional)
  • olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
Instruction
  1. Filling preparation

    Combine chopped onions, olive oil, tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, salt, and pepper. Cook for approximately 10 minutes until the liquid evaporates and the sauce thickens, then let it cool completely

  2. Dough preparation

    Mix milk, oil, an egg, and salt, then gradually add flour (450-500 g) until a ball forms. Divide the dough into 4 equal parts

  3. Layering (Puff pastry effect)

    Roll the dough into rectangles and brush with melted margarine. Stack the layers and perform successive folds while adding margarine between each fold, then let it rest in the fridge (repeat this process to obtain two blocks of pastry)

  4. Assembly

    Oil the baking pan and place the first layer of dough, pricking it with a fork. Spread the cooled filling evenly to the edges and cover it with the second layer of dough

  5. Finishing

    Brush the top with a glaze of egg yolk and milk, pre-cut the dough into squares, and place a black olive on each piece. Let it rest in the fridge for 10 minutes while the oven preheats

  6. Baking

    Preheat the oven to 200°C, then lower the temperature to 180C. Bake for approximately 30 minutes until the pastry is golden and flake
